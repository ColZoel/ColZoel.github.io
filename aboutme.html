About Me

Originally from Casper, Wyoming, I graduated from Brigham Young University with a B.S. in economics in 2023 where I served as the head computer vision developer at the Record Linking Lab at Brigham Young University. I led a team of 5-9 developers in building CV tools for genealogy.

Working with collaborators and clients such as FamilySearch, World Archives, the Wilford Woodruff Papers Foundation, and researchers from many notable universities, we leveraged custom BYU handwriting recognition models and segmentation classifiers to build a pipeline that inputs grid images (e.g. census records) and outputs a CSV transcription. I have presented at several conferences, including the BYU Family History Technology Workshop, and the exclusive President's Leadership Council, which is an annual event for the top 1% of BYU donors.

Current projects include a collaboration with Harvard Business School PhD candidate, Jonathan Palmer, in which I leverage BYU and open-source OCR tools to build a pipeline that inputs a directory of images of scanned city directories and outputs a tabular transcription. It then geocodes, maps, and then applies ML and spatial econometrics to get a sense of changes in professional networks, the propensity for entrepreneurship, and the overall impact of the Kodak crash on the economy in Rochester, New York. You can learn more about the project on the Research page.

Being entirely self-taught, I developed my technical skills from osmosis through my programmers, YouTube tutorials, online communities such as Stack Overflow, and a library of books I have accumulated on Python, machine learning, and data science. I have never taken formal coursework in computer science, but I am active in the international ML community.
